What it is

A full-stack company website I built as freelance work for Fantek — the same company I interned at as a Python Developer. When they needed a real web presence and an internal operations portal, they came to me. I built the whole thing solo.

This is not a side project. It's a live product that a real business runs on. That changes how you think about every decision.

The numbers and what they mean
Sole developer
I made every decision — architecture, database schema, UI, APIs. No team, no handoffs. When something broke, it was mine to fix.
Admin portal
full login system for internal team use — not just a public-facing site. Authentication, session management, role-based access.
PostgreSQL backend
relational schema storing all client requirements and operational data. Structured so it can feed directly into Power BI for live dashboards — that connection is in progress.
REST API integrations
lead capture and client inquiry workflows piped in through APIs, with data feeding into business review reports automatically.

What's different about client work

Personal projects have no consequences when they break. This one does. The login has to actually work. The data has to be secure. The client-facing side has to look professional enough that their customers trust it.

Building something a company depends on taught me more about architecture decisions than any tutorial — because every shortcut you take becomes a problem you have to explain to someone later.
