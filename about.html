how the coding started

Python in a boring class
is still Python.

It started at Velammal during 11th grade. Every class was boring — computer was the only one that was bearable. Me and my friend would sit there talking the whole time and still somehow get the highest marks. That's when I knew something was happening.

Python clicked in a way other languages didn't. Not Java, not C — Python. It made sense to me. It still does. That one bearable class in a school I hated became the thing I'm building a career on.

BCA happened because my mom wanted it to happen. And honestly it's been fine — data analytics, full stack, machine learning. I've built real things. But the long-term dream, the one I've had since 8th grade, is business. That's where I'm actually going, insha Allah. The tech is the foundation.

the stage thing

Give me a mic and
I'll own the room.

I've wanted to be in front of a microphone since I was a kid. Not performing — speaking. The kind of speaking where you hold a room, where people actually listen, where something shifts. I grew up watching student council members in schools abroad, parliamentary debates, political speeches — and I wanted that. All of it.

In college I've been Event Coordinator, NSS Vice President, and Department Secretary. Every role that had a stage, I took. Emcee at events is the closest I've found to what I actually want — the energy of holding a crowd, being the thread that ties an event together, making it fun without losing the weight of it.

"What I would do to get in front of a mic. Since I was a kid."

The dream was journalism or law. I'm political — genuinely, not just online. Up to date, opinionated, not quiet about it. I wanted to argue in courtrooms or break stories. That didn't happen the way I planned. But the voice is still there. It always finds a way out — through speeches, through poems, through this.
