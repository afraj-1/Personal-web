What it is

A full analytics platform I built while teaching myself data engineering — from raw data all the way to a live Power BI dashboard. The idea was to build something at real scale, not a toy dataset. So I designed it around 5,000+ purchase orders, 50 suppliers, and 4,800 shipments across five global regions.

It answers one question: which suppliers are actually reliable, and which are a risk? Every KPI, every model, every dashboard page points at that.

The numbers and what they mean
14,000+
records processed through the Python/Pandas pipeline. Not a sample — the whole thing, cleaned and structured.
8 KPIs
engineered from scratch — OTIF rate (On Time In Full), defect PPM, weighted supplier scores, sector weighting, and more. Each one is a calculated column, not a given field.
73.5%
accuracy on the Random Forest risk classifier. 17 features, trained on historical shipment data to flag high-risk suppliers before problems happen. Not perfect — but better than guessing.
World Bank API
integrated for real-time country-level LPI (Logistics Performance Index) and GDP data. Supplier risk scores factor in where the supplier is based, not just how they've performed.

What I actually learned

Mostly that real data work is 80% cleaning and 20% the interesting stuff. I built this to understand the full stack of analytics — not just running a model but designing the schema that feeds it, engineering the features it trains on, and presenting the output in a way a non-technical person could actually use.

The Power BI layer was harder than I expected. DAX is its own language. Getting the heat maps to show regional risk intuitively took more iterations than anything else.
