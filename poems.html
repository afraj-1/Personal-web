Written at 2am, mostly

Things I wrote
and kept.

These lived in my notes app for a while. They're not polished. They're not supposed to be. Putting them here anyway.

01
Buried Love
He asked for my heart. Did I just not give him my heart but also buried it under him. He allowed me to, though he knew I can't do the otherwise. Whose fault is it more, mine or him?I bent my boundaries into shapes that pleased him, hoping he'd see the art in my surrender. But instead, he rejected the shapes assuming those were the real ones.Love may not always sound loud when killing. But I'd hoped he would hear me. Now I know— He never listened.
02
The Pain of Fear
Every part of my body devours the pain your thoughts bring and I, rest it in me, smilingly.'Cause somehow, the pain feels better than not feeling anything.Maybe the fear— of feeling nothing for you, haunts me more. Or the fear of feeling empty is buried behind all my fears. I never know so shall you.
03
What about, Why?
What about the coldness that doesn't go away even with miles of sheets over you?What about the eyes that no longer can hold the weight?What about the cuts that burn deeper and deeper no matter how well hidden?What about the whispers that echo in your head when the solitude overshadows?And yet we breathe not knowing the answer to the numbness, the burden, the flames, the screams. But why?
04
This is new, and not.
The light in my eyes hesitate, again but not the same light.The curls of my lips twist, again but not the same twists.The pulse in my chest falters, again but not the same beat.The rhythm of my walk stall, again but not the same falls.I'm aware and accepting but not. This is new and not.
05
Die, but Not to Die
How does it feel? when death is staring at you and all you want to do is Kiss it—Not to stop breathing, but to feel someone else's breath. Not to stop breathing but to let go of the weight. Not to stop breathing but to stop running.So, How does it feel?
06
I come and go like a Dream
Maybe I will I will fly to your city. To touch you, to feel you, to let you hold me— and not ask if this is right.If this is right or wrong, kissing you in places I shouldn't be. Laughing at jokes, or making them too— I've wanted this forever. You did too.But when I leave, without a trace, will it matter? Without a promise, will it really matter if it was right or wrong, if I come and go like a dream? Will it matter to you?
07
Reality and Duality of Illusions
the reality and duality in my illusions, I hold you in silence, yet lose you in noise.you return like a ghost, touching my memory but not my skin, a wound that refuses to close, a fire that pretends to die.I hate the way I wait, for the hope of love. And now, where your desire leans toward me, yet your chains are fastened elsewhere— I burn in the space between.
08
My heart's home
Are you a home to my heart. it finds you when I am lost. You are the reason it knows what forever means.It tells the journey is thorny with an unknowing ending But yet, I take paths that lead to you.And when I see you smile, it smiles back even more. You are not just someone I love, you are the reason love feels real to me.
09
The House That Remembers
He sits where my childhood learned safety, hands red with answers he never speaks. I wake too late—as always— love arriving after the damage.The blade glints like a sentence he refuses to finish. I carry five years on my back and still try to kneel.My arms remember before my mind does. I wipe blood like it's rain, like tenderness can rewrite history.Somewhere, an older version of me is watching from the doorway, wondering how many times a heart can confuse mercy for fate.
10
After the War
I did not love him when I was whole. I loved him while something inside me was burning its own house.He was not always shelter. Sometimes he was the smoke. Sometimes the fire learned my name and answered.But I kept him anyway— like a song you hum to drown out the sound of breaking.I fought myself with his shadow on my back, with the thought of him stitched into my ribs, holding me upright when I wanted to fold.He was armor once. He was a wound too. I did not ask which was truer— both kept me alive.I loved him without language for future, without contracts or clean edges. Half-promises still glow when you are trying not to disappear. When the war ended, I returned carrying victory to a place that no longer knew my name.Nothing was stolen. Nothing was owed. Still, my hands were empty in the shape of him.I don't reach for him now. I reach for the echo— the proof that I once loved while bleeding and did not die. That is the pain. That is the miracle.
11
a thought, not a poem
He saved me, not him but the thoughts of him. And that's why unloving him feels like losing myself, at least a part of myself.
